Plasmonic Metasurfaces

My research on plasmonic metasurfaces focuses on the design and realization of functional optical interfaces using colloidal and large-area fabrication approaches. A central theme is the development of scalable nanoparticle assemblies that support collective plasmonic modes and enable strong light–matter interactions. Particular emphasis is placed on surface-enhanced spectroscopies, including SERS, where engineered architectures provide controlled electromagnetic hot-spots and reproducible enhancement platforms.

By combining bottom-up assembly with optical design principles, these metasurfaces are tailored for sensing, spectroscopy, and photonic applications, bridging the gap between fundamental plasmonics and device-level functionality.

Key directions:

  • Colloidal plasmonic superlattices and metasurfaces
  • Surface-enhanced Raman spectroscopy (SERS)
  • Hot-spot engineering and collective plasmon modes
  • Scalable nanophotonic interfaces

Hybrid Photonic–Plasmonic Systems

A key aspect of my research is the integration of plasmonic nanostructures with photonic platforms to achieve hybrid light–matter coupling. By interfacing metallic nanostructures with dielectric waveguides and resonant photonic modes, I explore the formation of hybrid plasmon–photon states that enhance optical confinement and spectral selectivity.

These systems enable new regimes of interaction, where localized plasmon resonances couple with guided or lattice modes, leading to enhanced spectroscopic signals and tunable optical responses. Such hybrid architectures are particularly relevant for sensing, nonlinear optics, and integrated photonic devices.

Key directions:

  • Guided-mode resonance coupled plasmonic systems
  • Hybrid plasmon–photon modes
  • Waveguide-integrated nanostructures
  • Angle-resolved spectroscopy

Chiral Nanophotonics

My work in chiral nanophotonics focuses on the design and fabrication of three-dimensional and planar chiral nanostructures for controlling light polarization and chiroptical responses. During my doctoral research, I developed interference lithography-based approaches for the realization of chiral photonic architectures, including woodpile and helical geometries.

More recently, I am extending these concepts toward colloidal and hybrid systems, aiming to achieve scalable chiral metasurfaces and plasmonic structures that exhibit strong circular dichroism and polarization-dependent optical responses.

Key directions:

  • Interference lithography for chiral nanostructures
  • Chiral plasmonic and photonic metasurfaces
  • Circular dichroism and polarization control
  • Colloidal approaches to chiral assemblies
