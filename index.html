Dr. Swagato Sarkar

Nanophotonics | Plasmonics | Colloidal Metasurfaces

I am an independent researcher in nano-optics and nanophotonics with expertise in plasmonic and photonic metasurfaces, hybrid light-matter interaction, and scalable bottom-up fabrication of photonic nanomaterials. My work focuses on collective plasmonic-photonic modes, symmetry-controlled optical response, and the development of large-area meta-optical interfaces using colloidal assembly and lithographic approaches.

Research Overview

My research develops functional optical nano-interfaces through a combination of bottom-up assembly, interference lithography, soft templating, and simulation-guided design. Across plasmonic metasurfaces, hybrid photonic-plasmonic systems, chiral nanophotonics, surface-enhanced spectroscopy, and functional nano-interfaces, the common objective is to create large-area, scalable architectures with tailored optical response and application relevance.

Main Scientific Themes

  • Plasmonic metasurfaces based on colloidal and soft-template fabrication pathways
  • Hybrid photonic-plasmonic systems for resonant light-matter interaction
  • Chiral nanophotonics and symmetry-controlled optical response
  • Surface-enhanced spectroscopy and collective mode engineering
  • Functional nano-interfaces for sensing, emission control, and multimodal devices
