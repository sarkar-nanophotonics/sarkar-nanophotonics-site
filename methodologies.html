Methodologies

Fabrication, characterization, and simulation workflow

My research combines scalable nanofabrication, optical and structural characterization, electromagnetic simulation, and data-driven analysis to establish structure-function relationships in complex photonic and plasmonic systems.

Nanofabrication Infrastructure

I work extensively with both bottom-up and lithographic nanofabrication approaches to develop large-area photonic and plasmonic metasurfaces.

  • Interference lithography: 325 nm, 442 nm, BlueMode, Kimmon Koha, SLM-based Holoeye LETO
  • Electron-beam lithography: Raith 150TWO, Raith eLine
  • Soft lithography and template-assisted fabrication
  • Thin-film deposition: e-beam, thermal, sputter, BETty
  • Cleanroom processing: Class 1000 to 10,000

Optical and Structural Characterization

Optical and structural characterization plays a central role in linking nanoscale geometry with resonant optical behavior and device functionality.

  • UV-Vis-NIR spectroscopy: Cary 5000
  • Bright-field and dark-field microscopy: Nikon Eclipse Ti-U, Keyence VHX
  • Polarization- and angle-resolved spectroscopy
  • Photoluminescence measurements
  • AFM: Bruker
  • SEM: JEOL JCM 6000
  • Ellipsometry: J.A. Woollam
  • Profilometry: Zeta

Advanced Optical Techniques

I also employ advanced optical techniques to probe emission dynamics and nanoscale light-matter interactions in complex photonic systems.

  • Confocal fluorescence microscopy
  • Time-resolved photoluminescence and TCSPC
